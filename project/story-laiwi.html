Los Hermanos Laiwi

Traditional legend · Province: Angaraes · Reading level: Secondary students

High in the mountains of Angaraes lived two siblings known as the Laiwi brothers. Their family took care of alpacas and llamas, and from a young age they learned to read the signs of the wind and the color of the sky.

One dry season, the river that crossed their community began to disappear. The elders said that an angered spirit was taking the water away because people had stopped respecting the mountains and throwing offerings into the river.

The Laiwi brothers decided to climb to the highest peak to ask for forgiveness and bring back the water. Along the way they met condors, foxes, and a small vizcacha that each gave them advice and simple gifts for the journey.
